# Vehicle Care OS (VCO) Imowash Reference Mapping

## Purpose

This document maps `Imowash` inspiration into concrete `VCO` product and UI decisions.

Reference project:
- Behance: https://www.behance.net/gallery/230970791/Fourmeta-Agency

## Reference Signals Observed

From the Behance project, the strongest signals are:
- premium vehicle care branding
- eco-aware positioning
- customer app presence
- service progress tracking
- membership or subscription plans
- website plus mobile product system
- strong typography and minimal UI structure

## Mapping From Imowash To VCO

## 1. Brand Positioning

Imowash signal:
- premium, clean, tech-forward vehicle care brand

VCO decision:
- frame VCO as `the operating system for modern vehicle care`

Why:
- this preserves the premium and digital posture while expanding beyond a single consumer brand into a platform business

## 2. Customer Product Experience

Imowash signal:
- customer-facing app screens for progress, subscriptions, and sign-in

VCO decision:
- include customer surfaces for:
- home
- book service
- my vehicles
- membership
- history
- account

Why:
- these are the minimum customer-facing surfaces needed to support the kind of experience Imowash implies

## 3. Memberships

Imowash signal:
- subscription screen and recurring-plan framing

VCO decision:
- make memberships a first-class MVP module with:
- plans
- subscriptions
- renewal visibility
- operator controls

Why:
- memberships are one of the strongest business differentiators in the reference

## 4. Live Service Visibility

Imowash signal:
- progress-style interface and service transparency

VCO decision:
- booking states include:
- pending
- confirmed
- assigned
- in progress
- ready
- completed

Why:
- customers should see service progression and operators should manage it in real time

## 5. Vehicle-Centric Service Model

Imowash signal:
- premium ongoing care relationship, not anonymous one-time transactions

VCO decision:
- make `vehicle` a first-class entity tied to:
- bookings
- history
- membership linkage where applicable
- notes and photos

Why:
- this makes VCO feel like a long-term care platform rather than a checkout form

## 6. Visual Language

Imowash signal:
- minimal layouts
- high contrast
- premium whitespace
- strong type hierarchy
- blue-black-white restrained palette

VCO decision:
- customer screens should use:
- clean visual hierarchy
- strong typographic contrast
- minimal clutter
- trust-oriented spacing and layout

Operator screens should use:
- denser information layout
- clear status chips
- structured panels
- less decorative treatment

Why:
- customer UX should inherit the premium feel, while operator UX should stay operationally efficient

## 7. Website Direction

Imowash signal:
- brand site and landing page feel integrated with the product

VCO decision:
- website should communicate:
- modern vehicle care platform
- memberships and repeat revenue
- service visibility
- operator control
- premium service experience

Why:
- VCO should not look like separate brand and product worlds

## 8. Internal Operations Expansion

Imowash signal:
- limited visible operational internals in the reference

VCO decision:
- expand with operator and technician layers:
- bookings dashboard
- CRM
- services management
- technician assignment
- checklists
- notes and media
- reports

Why:
- this is the difference between visual inspiration and a real software product

## 9. What To Mirror Closely

Mirror closely:
- the confidence of the brand
- the premium customer app feeling
- the emphasis on subscriptions
- the clean presentation of progress and service quality

## 10. What To Adapt Rather Than Mirror

Adapt rather than mirror:
- information architecture
- operator workflow depth
- technical architecture
- payments and reporting model
- multi-location logic

## 11. Recommended Design Principles For VCO

Use these principles when designing screens:
- customer experience should feel premium, calm, and trustworthy
- operator experience should feel fast, dense, and controllable
- technician experience should feel simple, durable, and task-first
- every major record should revolve around the vehicle and the booking
- memberships should be visible as a core growth engine, not a side feature

## 12. Recommended Next Design Pass

When designing VCO screens, use Imowash as inspiration for:
- customer home
- membership overview
- booking confirmation
- service status detail
- website hero and product storytelling

Use product logic from the VCO PRD for:
- operator dashboard
- booking management
- CRM
- payments
- technician workflow
- reports and settings

## Summary

`Imowash` should inform the feel and strategic framing of VCO.

`VCO` should go further by becoming the full operating system behind that experience.
